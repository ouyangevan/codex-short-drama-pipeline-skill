# Camera Rules

Use this compiled cache for stable lens, camera, light, composition, and professional direction rules before returning to the teaching database.

## Professional Direction

Every serious shot needs:

- dramatic function: reveal, conceal, pressure, intimacy, humiliation, suspicion, reversal, comedy beat, grief, temptation, proof, or cliffhanger
- viewer attention: first, second, and last eye target
- motivated camera: the move or stillness must change information, emotion, power, scale, or impact
- transition design: action cut, eye-line cut, reaction cut, object wipe, sound bridge, hard cut, J-cut, L-cut, foreground wipe, or another motivated transition
- lighting design: source, direction, contrast, color temperature, shadow, practical, rim/back light, silhouette, reflection, flare, or darkness reveal
- depth design: foreground occlusion, midground action, background information, reflection, door/window frame, corridor depth, crowd layer, or negative space

## Pressure And Tension

- Pressure is visual, not just narrative. Use low angle, foreground blocking, frame compression, looming silhouette, edge/bottom squeeze, shadow fall, screen-space imbalance, and scale dominance.
- Tension is visual timing. Use held pause, diagonal attack line, extreme-foreground threat, shallow-focus compression, occluded danger, slow push before burst, whip-pan anticipation, body coil, or near-contact gap.

## Large Scale And Spectacle

Large Eastern-aesthetic, fantasy, xianxia, or VFX shots need macro scale, foreground/midground/background layers, atmospheric depth, reveal path, environment response, and clear scale anchors.
